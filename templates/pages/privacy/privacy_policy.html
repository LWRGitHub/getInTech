Cookies Policy

To make the websites of Tech Career Time work properly, we sometimes place small data files called ‘cookies’ on your device.

What are cookies?

A cookie is a small text file that is stored on your computer or mobile device by a website during your visit. It allows the website to recall your actions and preferences (such as login details, language, font size, and other display settings) for a duration of time, thereby eliminating the need to re-enter these details each time you revisit the site or navigate between pages.

  • First-party cookies are those placed by the website you are visiting and can only be read by that specific site. Additionally, a website may utilize external services that set their own cookies, referred to as third-party cookies.
  • Persistent cookies are stored on your computer and remain intact even after you close your browser, unlike session cookies, which are automatically deleted when you end your browsing session.

When you access the Tech Career Time website from an IP address that we detects is in a data-restricted region, you will receive a prompt to either accept or refuse cookies. Should this prompt not appear, default settings compliant with regional data protection laws will automatically apply.

Cookies can also be used to establish anonymised statistics about the browsing experience on our sites.

How do we use cookies?

Tech Career Time utilizes various Google products, including but not limited to Google AdSense and Google Analytics, to enhance our site's functionality, user experience, enhance our outreach and continuously improve all aspects.

Google Analytics and Google AdSense use cookies in distinct ways to help websites analyze user behavior and monetize their content, respectively. Here’s a detailed look at how each uses cookies:

Google Analytics Cookies

Google Analytics is a tool that helps website owners understand how visitors interact with their website. It uses cookies to collect information and report website usage statistics without personally identifying individual visitors to Google. Here are the key points about Google Analytics cookies:

  • Tracking Visitor Behavior: Google Analytics cookies are used to track a user’s interactions with a website, such as the pages they visit, the amount of time they spend on each page, and the actions they take.
  • Types of Cookies: The most common Google Analytics cookies are _ga, _gid, and _gat. The _ga cookie is used to distinguish users and has a default expiration of 2 years. The _gid cookie also distinguishes users but expires after 24 hours. The _gat cookie is used to throttle the request rate, limiting the collection of data on high traffic sites, and it expires after 1 minute.
  • Purpose: These cookies help website owners collect data on user behavior, which can be used to improve the website's performance and the user experience.

Google AdSense Cookies

Google AdSense is an advertising placement service by Google that uses cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to your website or other websites on the internet. Here’s how AdSense uses cookies:

  • Targeted Advertising: AdSense cookies are used to serve ads based on a user's previous visits to websites that use Google advertising services. This is known as interest-based advertising.
  • Types of Cookies: Common cookies used by Google AdSense include _gads and _gac. These cookies are used to measure interactions with the ads on that domain and prevent the same ads from being shown too many times.
  • Purpose: These cookies allow Google AdSense to display ads that are relevant to the user, thus providing more targeted advertising that can be more engaging to users and potentially more profitable for publishers.

Both Google Analytics and Google AdSense cookies are critical for website owners who rely on data and advertising revenue.

In regions subject to data restrictions, when a cookie consent popup is presented, we collect the necessary data based on your consent preferences to ascertain your choices. If no popup appears, we set cookies in accordance with local data protection laws.

Third-Party Cookies

Some pages on our site feature content from external providers, such as YouTube. Additionally, there are links to external websites over which we have no control. Users are advised to exercise discretion before navigating to a new website.

Manage Cookies?

You have the ability to manage or delete cookies as desired. For more information, please visit aboutcookies.org.

Removing Cookies from Your Device:

You can delete all existing cookies on your device by clearing the browsing history in your browser. This action will remove cookies from all websites you have visited.

Managing Site-Specific Cookies:

For more detailed control over site-specific cookies, you can check the privacy and cookie settings in your preferred browser.

Blocking Cookies:

Most modern browsers can be configured to block cookies from being placed on your device. However, you may need to manually adjust your preferences each time you visit a website or a specific page. Additionally, some services and functionalities, such as logging into your profile, may not function properly without cookies.
